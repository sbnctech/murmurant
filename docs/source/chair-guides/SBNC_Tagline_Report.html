SBNC Tagline Ballot Results Report

Prepared for: SBNC Communications & Membership Committees

Purpose: Evaluate community endorsement of five potential taglines using a weighted ranking ballot.

1. Overview of the Ballot Process

Members were asked to rank five candidate taglines. Points were assigned using a 3–2–1 weighted system:

  • 1st choice = 3 points
  • 2nd choice = 2 points
  • 3rd choice = 1 point

Total scores reflect aggregate preference strength, not the number of voters.

The five tested taglines were:

  1. “The Community that makes Santa Barbara feel like home.”
  2. “Connections that make Santa Barbara feel like home.”
  3. “Connect. Belong. Thrive in Santa Barbara.”
  4. “Find your people. Feel at home in SB — fast.”
  5. “New City, new friends, nonstop fun.”

5. Interpretation of Quantitative Results

5.1 Clear Winner

“Connect. Belong. Thrive in Santa Barbara.”

  • Highest total score (34)
  • Largest number of first-place points
  • Strong performance across all three ranking tiers
  • Resonates broadly and deeply with the respondents

This tagline would “win” under multiple reasonable voting systems (Borda-style scoring, simple plurality, or ranked choice), which indicates a robust underlying preference, not a fluke of the scoring method.

5.2 Strong Runner-Up

“New City, new friends, nonstop fun.”

  • Second-highest score (32)
  • Very strong in 1st- and 3rd-place points
  • More energetic and youth-oriented tone than the winner
  • Appeals to newcomers who prioritize social activity and rapid integration

This tagline has a different personality from the winner but is consistently favored, suggesting a meaningful subgroup that responds strongly to a high-energy pitch.

5.3 Solid Third Place

“The Community that makes Santa Barbara feel like home.”

  • Strong second-place support
  • More traditional, community-minded tone
  • Likely to appeal to members who value stability, continuity, and a “home base” feeling

5.4 Weak Support for the Remaining Taglines

  • “Connections that make Santa Barbara feel like home.” is very similar in concept to “The Community that makes Santa Barbara feel like home.” but tested weaker, suggesting redundancy and vote-splitting with a less compelling phrasing.
  • “Find your people. Feel at home in SB — fast.” is action-oriented and emphasizes speed, but the “fast” promise may feel transactional or superficial for some respondents.

Both trailing taglines underperform across all three ranking tiers, indicating that they are unlikely to be competitive finalists.

6. Patterns and Insights Beyond the Numbers

6.1 Two Clear Clusters Emerge

The five taglines fall into two thematic families based on content and tone.

Cluster A — Belonging & Community (Warmer, Broader Appeal)
Taglines: “The Community that makes Santa Barbara feel like home.”, “Connections that make Santa Barbara feel like home.”, “Connect. Belong. Thrive in Santa Barbara.”

Traits:

  • Emphasize emotional grounding and a sense of home
  • Speak to deeper human needs: connection, belonging, community
  • Frame SBNC as more than a social calendar — as a real community

Performance:

  • “Connect. Belong. Thrive in Santa Barbara.” clearly dominates this cluster by using modern, active verbs and a concise three-part structure.
  • “The Community that makes Santa Barbara feel like home.” performs respectably but with a softer, more traditional feel.
  • “Connections that make Santa Barbara feel like home.” appears to be a weaker variant of the same idea, diluting support without adding distinct value.

Conclusion:
➤ Voters prefer a modern, concise, active version of the belonging/community message, which is captured best by “Connect. Belong. Thrive in Santa Barbara.”

Cluster B — Fun & Energy (Social Momentum)
Taglines: “Find your people. Feel at home in SB — fast.”, “New City, new friends, nonstop fun.”

Traits:

  • Emphasize speed, activity, and enthusiasm
  • Convey that SBNC helps newcomers get “plugged in” quickly
  • Use a more casual, youthful, and high-energy tone

Performance:

  • “New City, new friends, nonstop fun.” is a strong second overall, showing genuine demand for a fun-first, social-energy message.
  • “Find your people. Feel at home in SB — fast.” likely loses some respondents with the explicit emphasis on speed (“fast”), which can sound more transactional than relational.

Conclusion:
➤ When respondents favor a fun-first tagline, “New City, new friends, nonstop fun.” clearly captures that sentiment more effectively than the alternatives.

6.2 Why “Connect. Belong. Thrive in Santa Barbara.” Wins Across the Board

The winning tagline successfully blends the emotional grounding of Cluster A with some of the forward motion and optimism associated with Cluster B.

  • Not too formal, not too breezy
  • Flexible for multiple audiences and age groups
  • Aligned with the Club’s mission: helping newcomers settle, connect, and flourish in Santa Barbara
  • Short, memorable, and visually clean for use in headers, banners, and digital layouts

In other words, “Connect. Belong. Thrive in Santa Barbara.” communicates both belonging and momentum — which is exactly what newcomers are looking for when they decide whether SBNC will help Santa Barbara feel like home.

6.3 Strategic Implication for SBNC

The winning tagline is not only the top-rated; it is also strategically versatile:

  • Works across demographics (20s, 30s, retirees, job relocations, life transitions)
  • Compatible with SBNC’s historic positioning as a welcoming, community-oriented organization
  • Supports both social and community-focused messaging campaigns
  • Easy to reuse as a unifying phrase in email marketing, web content, and print materials

“New City, new friends, nonstop fun.” can be retained as an alternate “youth + social energy” subtagline for specific contexts (e.g., newcomers’ events, mixers, or campaigns focused on younger cohorts).

7. Recommendation

Primary Tagline for SBNC

➡️ “Connect. Belong. Thrive in Santa Barbara.”

Optional Secondary / Context-Specific Tagline

➡️ “New City, new friends, nonstop fun.”

This pairing allows the club to:

  • Maintain a strong, universal brand message centered on connection and belonging
  • Use a more energetic, fun-forward secondary message when appropriate
  • Appeal to both major motivational clusters identified in the ballot results

8. Conclusion

The ballot results clearly show that “Connect. Belong. Thrive in Santa Barbara.” is the preferred choice, with strong preference depth, broad appeal across ranking tiers, and thematic alignment with SBNC’s mission and values.

Patterns across the taglines reveal two distinct motivational clusters among respondents—Belonging/Community and Fun/Energy—with the winning tagline excelling because it effectively bridges both. This gives SBNC a strong, flexible foundation for future communications and branding efforts.
