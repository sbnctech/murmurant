# Competitive Analysis: Wild Apricot and Market Landscape

**Last updated:** 2025-12-28

---

## Executive Summary

Wild Apricot dominates the small-to-medium membership organization market, but large national organizations (Rotary, Lions, BSA) operate proprietary centralized systems. ClubOS targets the underserved middle tier: independent clubs and local chapters that need more than Wild Apricot offers but don't have access to national-level platforms.

---

## Market Landscape

### Tier 1: Centralized National Systems

Large membership organizations with proprietary platforms managed at the national level:

| Organization | Platform | Notes |
|--------------|----------|-------|
| Rotary International | DACdb / ClubRunner | Centralized member database, club reporting |
| Lions Clubs International | Lion Portal | Global membership management |
| Boy Scouts of America | Scoutbook | Unit-level tracking, advancement |
| Kiwanis International | Kiwanis Connect | Club and district management |

**Key characteristics:**
- Mandated by national organization
- No market choice for local units
- Deep integration with national reporting
- NOT addressable market for ClubOS

### Tier 2: Chapter-Level Solutions

Chapters of larger organizations that have autonomy over their technology choices:

| Organization Type | Example Orgs | Current Solutions |
|-------------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Professional associations | IREM, ATD, SHRM chapters | Wild Apricot, MemberClicks |
| Alumni groups | University clubs | Wild Apricot, custom |
| Trade associations | Local builder associations | GrowthZone, Wild Apricot |

**Key characteristics:**
- Local chapter autonomy
- Budget constraints ($50-500/mo range)
- Often using Wild Apricot or similar
- Addressable with right positioning

### Tier 3: Independent Clubs

Standalone organizations with no national affiliation:

| Club Type | Examples | Current Solutions |
|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| Newcomer/social clubs | Santa Barbara Newcomers | Wild Apricot, spreadsheets |
| Hobby clubs | Garden clubs, car clubs | Wild Apricot, Facebook groups |
| Community groups | Neighborhood associations | Spreadsheets, nothing |

**Key characteristics:**
- Full technology autonomy
- Highly price-sensitive
- Often volunteer-run
- **Primary ClubOS target market**

## Wild Apricot Deep Dive

### Market Position

- **Customer count:** 15,000-26,000+ organizations (estimates vary by source)
- **Parent company:** Personify (acquired 2017)
- **Market position:** Dominant in small-to-medium membership organizations

### Pricing (as of 2024)

| Tier | Contacts | Price/Month |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Free | Up to 50 | $0 |
| Personal | Up to 100 | $60 |
| Group | Up to 250 | $75 |
| Community | Up to 500 | $140 |
| Professional | Up to 2,000 | $240 |
| Network | Up to 5,000 | $440 |
| Enterprise | Up to 15,000 | $530 |
| Global Leader | Up to 50,000 | $900 |

### Named Customers

- **Well-Read Mom** - Catholic women's reading groups (~10,000 members across chapters)
- **IREM chapters** - Institute of Real Estate Management local chapters
- **ATD chapters** - Association for Talent Development local chapters
- Various newcomer clubs, garden clubs, professional associations

### Strengths

1. **Market leader** - Brand recognition, established trust
2. **Free tier** - Low barrier to entry for small clubs
3. **Comprehensive feature set** - Events, membership, payments, website
4. **Self-service** - No sales process for smaller tiers
5. **Ecosystem** - Integrations, add-ons, consultant network

### Weaknesses

1. **Complexity** - Steep learning curve for volunteers
2. **Expensive at scale** - $530+/mo for larger organizations
3. **Weak permissions model** - Limited role-based access control
4. **No activity groups** - Interest groups are a workaround, not first-class
5. **Poor event coordination** - No carpool, potluck, or volunteer coordination
6. **Aging UX** - Interface shows its age
7. **Limited customization** - Rigid workflows, hard to adapt

## ClubOS Positioning

### Target Market

**Primary:** Independent social and newcomer clubs (Tier 3)
- 50-500 members
- Volunteer-run
- Activity/interest group focused
- Event-heavy calendars
- Price-sensitive ($50-150/mo sweet spot)

**Secondary:** Chapter-level organizations with autonomy (Tier 2)
- Local chapters of professional associations
- Alumni groups
- Trade association chapters

### Differentiation from Wild Apricot

| Dimension | Wild Apricot | ClubOS |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| **Activity groups** | Workaround (member groups) | First-class feature |
| **Event coordination** | Basic RSVP | Carpool, potluck, volunteers |
| **Permissions** | Coarse roles | Capability-based RBAC |
| **UX complexity** | High (power users) | Volunteer-friendly |
| **Pricing model** | Per-contact | Aligned with club value |
| **Customization** | Limited | Flexible workflows |

### Key Value Propositions

1. **Purpose-built for social clubs** - Not adapted from association software
2. **Activity groups as first-class** - Not a workaround
3. **Event coordination tools** - Carpool matching, potluck signup, volunteer coordination
4. **Volunteer-friendly UX** - Designed for rotating leadership
5. **Aligned pricing** - Priced for club budgets, not enterprise associations
6. **Modern architecture** - Built on current technology stack

### Competitive Response Strategy

**When competing against Wild Apricot:**
- Emphasize activity group support
- Demonstrate volunteer-friendly UX
- Show event coordination features
- Calculate total cost at their member count

**When competing against Join It / simpler tools:**
- Emphasize comprehensive feature set
- Show activity group and committee support
- Demonstrate event management depth

### Wild Apricot "Stripes" Layout System

**Architecture Constraint:**
Wild Apricot sites use a **stripe-based layout system** where pages are composed of horizontal full-width sections stacked vertically. This creates:

- Uniform horizontal bands across the page
- Limited grid flexibility within stripes
- Predictable but rigid page structures
- All consultant themes work within this constraint

**ClubOS Advantage:**
Our block-based CMS is NOT restricted to stripes. We can offer:

| Capability | Wild Apricot | ClubOS |
|------------|--------------|--------|
| Multi-column layouts | Limited within stripes | Full grid flexibility |
| Asymmetric grids | No | Yes |
| Sidebar + content | Theme-level only | Page-level choice |
| Magazine layouts | No | Yes |
| Card arrangements | Fixed patterns | Flexible placement |
| Nested blocks | No | Yes |

This architectural difference enables layouts that Wild Apricot simply cannot achieve regardless of consultant customization.
