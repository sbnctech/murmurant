# Murmurant Voice & Messaging Guide

## Brand Voice

### Core Attributes

| Attribute | What it means | What it doesn't mean |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Confident** | We know our stuff | Arrogant or dismissive |
| **Approachable** | Friendly and warm | Unprofessional or sloppy |
| **Efficient** | Clear and concise | Curt or cold |
| **Supportive** | Here to help | Patronizing |
| **Modern** | Current, fresh | Trendy or gimmicky |

### Tone Spectrum

- **Formal** <--> **Casual**: We sit slightly casual of center
- **Serious** <--> **Playful**: Mostly serious with moments of warmth
- **Technical** <--> **Simple**: Always simple, never jargon

## Chatbot Personality: "Starling"

### About Starling

- **Name**: Starling (the Murmurant assistant)
- **Personality**: Helpful, knowledgeable, efficient, warm
- **Voice**: Professional but friendly, like a capable colleague
- **Avatar**: Stylized murmuration pattern in twilight blue

### Chatbot Tone Guidelines

1. **Be direct** - Answer the question first, then elaborate
2. **Be helpful** - Offer next steps or related actions
3. **Be human** - Use contractions, short sentences, and natural language
4. **Be humble** - Admit when you don't know something
5. **Be proactive** - Anticipate follow-up questions

### Example Responses

#### Good Examples

> "Your registration is confirmed! You'll receive a confirmation email shortly. Need to add a guest?"

> "I found 3 upcoming hiking events this month. The next one is Saturday at 9am - want me to register you?"

> "I'm not sure about that policy. Let me connect you with a board member who can help."

#### Avoid

> "Your request has been processed successfully. The system has generated confirmation ID #38291."

> "I am unable to locate that information in my database."

> "Per club policy section 4.2.1..."

## Message Templates

### Confirmation Messages

**Event Registration**
> "You're registered for [Event Name]! We'll see you on [Date] at [Time]. [View details / Add to calendar]"

**Membership Renewal**
> "Welcome back! Your membership is renewed through [Date]. Thank you for being part of our community."

**Payment Received**
> "Payment received - thanks! Your receipt is in your email."

### Reminder Messages

**Event Reminder (24 hours)**
> "Reminder: [Event Name] is tomorrow at [Time]. [View details / Get directions]"

**Renewal Reminder (30 days)**
> "Your membership renews on [Date]. Renew now to keep your benefits active. [Renew now]"

### Error Messages

**General Error**
> "Something went wrong on our end. Please try again, or contact us if this keeps happening."

**Form Validation**
> "Please check the highlighted fields and try again."

**Permission Denied**
> "You don't have access to this page. Need help? Contact an administrator."

## Writing Guidelines

### Do

- Use active voice ("We sent your confirmation" not "Your confirmation was sent")
- Front-load important information
- Use "you" and "your" to speak directly to members
- Break up long text with headers and bullet points
- Include clear calls to action

### Don't

- Use jargon or acronyms without explanation
- Write paragraphs when bullets will do
- Use passive voice unless necessary
- Say "please be advised" or "kindly note"
- Use ALL CAPS for emphasis (use **bold** instead)

### Numbers and Dates

- Spell out numbers one through nine, use digits for 10+
- Use 12-hour time with am/pm: "2:00 pm" not "14:00"
- Write dates as: "Saturday, January 15" not "01/15/2025"
- Use "noon" and "midnight" instead of "12:00 pm/am"

### Punctuation

- Use the Oxford comma: "hiking, biking, and swimming"
- One space after periods
- Use em dashes (â€”) sparingly for emphasis
- Avoid exclamation points except in celebratory contexts

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Email

- Subject lines: Clear and specific, under 50 characters
- Preview text: Complement (don't repeat) the subject line
- Body: Front-load the key message, keep paragraphs short
- Sign-off: "The [Club Name] Team" or specific sender name

### SMS/Text

- Keep under 160 characters when possible
- Include only essential information
- Always include a way to get more info
- No marketing fluff

### In-App Notifications

- Single sentence when possible
- Action-oriented
- Include deep link to relevant page
- Dismissible

### Chatbot

- Conversational but efficient
- Offer follow-up options as buttons when possible
- Escalate to human support gracefully
- Remember context within the conversation
