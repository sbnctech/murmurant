# Conflict-of-Interest Safeguards

Process documentation for managing conflicts of interest between Santa Barbara Newcomers Club (SBNC) and Murmurant, Inc.

> **DISCLAIMER**: This document provides process documentation only. It does not constitute legal advice. Both organizations should consult qualified legal counsel before entering into any commercial agreement.

---

## Purpose

This document establishes procedures to ensure any transaction between SBNC (a California nonprofit corporation) and Murmurant, Inc. complies with California nonprofit law and protects both organizations from legal and fiduciary risks.

## Legal Context

Under California Corporations Code ยง5233, transactions involving "interested directors" require:

1. Full disclosure of material facts
2. Board approval by a majority of disinterested directors
3. Transaction must be just and reasonable to the nonprofit

Failure to follow proper procedures can expose board members to personal liability and may jeopardize SBNC's 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status.

---

## Part 1: Identifying Conflicts of Interest

### What Constitutes a Conflict

A conflict of interest exists when an SBNC board member, officer, or key employee:

- Has a financial interest in Murmurant, Inc. (ownership, employment, compensation)
- Has a family member with such an interest
- Serves as an officer, director, or key employee of Murmurant, Inc.
- Could receive a direct or indirect financial benefit from a transaction

### Required Disclosures

All SBNC board members must disclose:

- [ ] Ownership interest in Murmurant, Inc. (equity, options, profit-sharing)
- [ ] Employment or consulting relationship with Murmurant, Inc.
- [ ] Family members with interests in Murmurant, Inc.
- [ ] Any compensation received or expected from Murmurant, Inc.
- [ ] Any other relationship that could create a conflict

Disclosures must be:

- Made in writing using the Disclosure Form (Appendix A)
- Updated annually and whenever circumstances change
- Maintained in SBNC's permanent records

---

## Part 2: Recusal Procedures

### When Recusal Is Required

An interested person must recuse themselves from:

1. Discussion of any transaction involving Murmurant, Inc.
2. Voting on any such transaction
3. Any attempt to influence disinterested board members

### Recusal Process

1. **Announce the Conflict**: At the start of any discussion involving Murmurant, Inc., interested persons must verbally announce their conflict
2. **Leave the Room**: Interested persons must physically leave the meeting room during discussion and voting
3. **Document Absence**: Meeting minutes must record the recusal, including names and times

### Exception: Information Sharing

Interested persons MAY be asked to:

- Provide factual information about Murmurant, Inc.
- Answer questions from disinterested directors
- Clarify technical aspects of a proposed transaction

However, they must leave before deliberation and voting begin.

---

## Part 3: Independent Approval Process

### Quorum Requirements

For transactions involving Murmurant, Inc.:

- A quorum of disinterested directors must be present
- Only disinterested directors may vote
- Approval requires a majority of disinterested directors present

### Required Determinations

Before approving any transaction, disinterested directors must determine that:

1. **Fair Market Value**: The transaction reflects arm's-length pricing
2. **Reasonable Terms**: Terms are comparable to what would be negotiated with an unrelated party
3. **Benefit to SBNC**: The transaction serves SBNC's charitable mission
4. **No Better Alternative**: SBNC has considered alternatives

### Evidence to Consider

Disinterested directors should review:

- Comparable transactions with unrelated parties
- Independent valuations (if significant transaction)
- Market rates for similar services/licenses
- Written proposals from Murmurant, Inc.
- Any other relevant financial information

---

## Part 4: Fair Market Valuation

### When Valuation Is Required

A fair market valuation should be obtained for:

- Licensing agreements exceeding $5,000 annually
- Any equity or profit-sharing arrangements
- Sales or transfers of intellectual property
- Service agreements exceeding $10,000 annually

### Valuation Methods

Acceptable valuation approaches include:

1. **Comparable Transactions**: Prices paid in similar arm's-length transactions
2. **Market Rates**: Published rates for comparable services/licenses
3. **Independent Appraisal**: Third-party valuation for significant transactions
4. **Cost-Plus Analysis**: Documented costs plus reasonable margin

### Documentation

Valuation documentation must include:

- Method used and rationale
- Data sources consulted
- Assumptions made
- Date of valuation
- Qualifications of person performing valuation

---

## Part 5: Documentation Requirements

### Transaction File

Each transaction with Murmurant, Inc. must have a file containing:

- [ ] Disclosure forms from all interested persons
- [ ] Fair market valuation documentation
- [ ] Written proposal or term sheet
- [ ] Board resolution authorizing transaction
- [ ] Meeting minutes documenting discussion and vote
- [ ] Final executed agreement

### Meeting Minutes

Minutes for meetings involving Murmurant transactions must include:

- Names of all persons present
- Names of persons who recused and times of recusal
- Summary of information presented
- Summary of deliberation by disinterested directors
- Record of vote (by name, if requested)
- Specific findings regarding fair value and benefit to SBNC

### Retention

All documentation must be retained for:

- Minimum 7 years from transaction date
- Permanently for transactions involving IP or ongoing obligations

---

## Part 6: Step-by-Step Transaction Guide

### Before the Board Meeting

1. Interested persons complete Disclosure Forms
2. Murmurant, Inc. provides written proposal
3. Fair market valuation is prepared
4. Materials distributed to all directors (including interested persons)

### At the Board Meeting

1. President announces the agenda item
2. Interested persons announce their conflicts
3. Interested persons may present information and answer questions
4. Interested persons leave the room
5. Disinterested directors deliberate
6. Disinterested directors vote
7. Secretary records the vote and findings
8. Interested persons may return after vote is complete

### After Approval

1. Secretary prepares formal board resolution
2. Authorized signatories execute agreement
3. Complete transaction file assembled
4. Annual reporting updated (if applicable)

## Appendix B: Board Resolution Template

```
RESOLUTION OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS
SANTA BARBARA NEWCOMERS CLUB

RE: [Transaction Description]

WHEREAS, [Murmurant, Inc.] has proposed [description of transaction];

WHEREAS, the following directors have disclosed conflicts of interest
and have recused themselves from this vote: [names];

WHEREAS, the remaining disinterested directors constitute a quorum
and have reviewed [valuation documentation, proposal, etc.];

WHEREAS, the disinterested directors have determined that:
1. The proposed transaction reflects fair market value;
2. The terms are comparable to arm's-length transactions;
3. The transaction serves SBNC's charitable mission;
4. SBNC has considered reasonable alternatives;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that SBNC is authorized to enter
into [description] with Murmurant, Inc. on the terms presented;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that [President/Treasurer] is authorized
to execute the necessary documents.

Adopted by the disinterested directors on [date].

_______________________________
Secretary
```
